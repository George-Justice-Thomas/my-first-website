The George Lore

I enjoy simple things and the like. After graduating from Dwight Morrow High School I went to Rutgers Newark unsure of what I wanted to major in. Feeling like the college life wasn't right for me, I decided to leave college and join the work force.

I worked for many different places, here is my list of my top 3 jobs I have worked at in the past:

  1. Tape Press Operator
  2. Baker
  3. Barista

That being said,my favorite was the tape press warehouse in Bergenfield, where we would print logos on rolls of tape.

I started off learning as a press operator assistant, "P.O.I.N.T." it was called. due to my intrigue in the press machines themselves I sought help from operators who I had become friends with to teach me during off time. I was then assigned to a new machine and had finally become what I had been working towards.

I was proud to be a machine operator and the ability to have a schedule that went by as fast as you pushed the product out was something I enjoyed. But this job gave me the same problem that other jobs in the past have provided: anxiety. And a lot of it. After work days I found myself socially drained, nervous of my decisions and how they would impact me, a feeling of inadequacy for feeling these feelings, and an overall lack of enthusiasm from me.

Cut to now, where I've found an interest in learning to develop software and write code. It feels like a spark is lit somewhere inside my heart. I feel the material to be immensely rewarding and a remote environment that retains a cooperative staff through virtual calls is something I believe I would benefit greatly from.
